Why is LaserSoft Struggling?

One of my favorite German sayings is "Der Fisch stinkt vom Kopf" (The fish stinks from the head, Рыба гниёт с головы). The fate of LaserSoft is largely due to the product strategy of their president, Karl-Heinz Zahorsky.

He's a nice guy - I've met him, I like him, I used to talk to him on the phone every month or two and he has excellent engineers working for him. However, the company strategy didn't adapt with the times.

The strategy for SilverFast has been to make expensive software for expensive scanners and make the majority of revenues from bundling this software with scanners.

Unfortunately, scanner technology reached it's peak 5 years ago. There's seldom any need for reflective scans higher than 600 dpi, and the best film scanners ever made were released by Nikon 8 years ago (CoolScan 5000 and CoolScan V).

95% of all scanners sold today are multi-function printer/scanner/copiers, most notably from Canon, Epson and HP. Nikon stopped making film scanners years ago, and now you can only buy used Nikon scanners on ebay. No other high-quality film scanners are sold any more because the market for film scanners is rapidly shrinking.

Anyone who primarly does reflective scans can buy a good printer/scanner/copier for $100, and anyone still scanning film can use the Epson V700 to do this. The problem LaserSoft has is that they don't support any printer/scanner/copiers and nobody needs SilverFast with Epson scanners (Epson Scan is actually better).

This leads to a dilemma - the market for the scanners SilverFast supports is shrinking rapidly, and even the least expensive printer/scanner/copiers are more than good enough for 99% of reflective scanning. What can LaserSoft do, other than try to reduce costs by laying off engineers and delay new product development? It's been 5 years since Intel Macs were introduced, and LaserSoft still hasn't released a universal binary version of SilverFast.

The majority of LaserSoft revenues used to come from bundled software sales, but Nikon doesn't make scanners any more, Microtek went out of business, Minolta went out of business, Epson Scan is better than SilverFast, Canon sells many more printer/scanner/copiers than high-end flatbed scanners, HP's G4050 isn't very good, Plustek and Reflecta scanners aren't very good, and the expensive scanners previously sold by Heidelberg are obsolete.

In addition, the SilverFast product pricing isn't competitive. There are two approaches - low cost and high volume or high cost and low volume. LaserSoft has chosen the latter, but high cost doesn't work when good reflective scanners cost $100 and film scanning can be done better with VueScan or Epson Scan.

LaserSoft has also annoyed their users for years, never releasing a Universal Binary version on the Mac, poor support for Windows x64, high prices, needing to buy another copy of SilverFast for each scanner, and generally not listening to their users.

What can LaserSoft do to survive? They don't ask for my advice, and free advice is worth what you pay for it. :)

However, Hamrick Software's strategy is to focus on making high-quality software that's easy to use and that adds value over the free software that comes with most scanners today. We're also committed to keeping our prices low and expanding into new markets (printer/scanner/copiers and iPhone and iPad).

We also think our development strategy is superior to LaserSoft's, since we do frequent, incremental product improvements - and have 4000 people test these improved versions every day. This leads to higher product quality than SilverFast, which only has significant new releases every 5 years or so.

In the end, the success or failure of a company is determined by product strategy and product quality. We're happy with the product strategy for VueScan and the quality of VueScan, and we hope more and more SilverFast users will agree.

Regards,

Ed Hamrick
